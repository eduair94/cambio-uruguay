- **Author:** eduair94 (with Claude Code)

---

## 1. Summary

A new standalone, SEO-focused landing page that answers, for **foreign tourists**, the question *"where and how do I get cash in Uruguay?"* It covers ATM networks, USD-vs-pesos withdrawal, fees/limits, the dynamic-currency-conversion trap, a curated directory of withdrawal/exchange spots in the top tourist zones, casas de cambio vs ATM vs bank, how much cash to carry, the tourist IVA benefit, and safety.

The page is **fully trilingual** (es default, en, pt) with localized URLs and hreflang. Content is grounded in dedicated web research (June 2026) with explicit source and staleness notes.

## 2. Goals

- Rank for high-intent tourist queries: `withdraw cash Uruguay`, `ATM Uruguay tourist`, `sacar dinheiro Uruguai`, `retirar efectivo Uruguay`, `cajeros Uruguay turistas`.
- Be genuinely useful and accurate; avoid stale or invented specifics.
- Reinforce the site's internal link graph (nav, footer, related guides, `/sucursales`) and funnel toward the comparador.

## 3. Audience & scope

- **Primary:** foreign tourists with a Visa/Mastercard debit/credit card (arriving from Argentina, Brazil, USA/Europe).
- **Non-goals:** resident/local banking products, prepaid local cards (Prex), live ATM map / geolocation, crypto, real-time fee scraping.

## 6. Content sections

Ordered for tourist intent and SEO. Each fact below carries the research confidence and a source; staleness-prone numbers are soft-framed in copy with a "verify on arrival / last checked" cue.

### 6.1 Hero + TL;DR box
Quick answer card: Uruguay ATMs dispense **both US dollars and pesos** (an edge over Argentina); two interconnected networks; **decline currency conversion** (take local currency); pay large bills by card for the tax benefit; skip airport/ferry exchange for big sums.

### 6.2 The two ATM networks
- RedBROU (state bank BROU's network, 400+ machines) and Banred (private-bank network, 370+), interconnected; both link internationally via Cirrus/Maestro/Visa/Plus/Link. *[High]*
- Look for the Cirrus/Maestro/Visa/Plus logos to confirm a machine takes foreign cards. *[High]*
- Carry both a Visa **and** a Mastercard as redundancy. *[Medium — anecdotal BROU/Mastercard quirk, framed as "bring both," not a current rule]*
- Source: guruguay.com/atm-problems-uruguay (updated 2025-03-26); brou.com.uy; banred.com.uy.

### 6.3 USD or pesos?
- Both dispensed; USD typically in $100 bills (interacts with low USD caps → may get a single note). *[High for USD-dispensing; Medium for "$100 only"]*
- Guidance on when to take USD vs pesos. Source: guruguay.com/exchanging-money-in-uruguay.

### 6.4 Fees, limits & DCC
- Per-transaction caps for foreign cards: **Banred ~USD 300 / ~5,000 UYU; RedBROU ~USD 200 / ~5,000 UYU** — set by the local network, not your bank. *[Medium-High; single reputable source — copy says "approximate, verify on arrival"]*
- Daily ceiling usually set by your **home bank** (~USD 500/day common) — call your bank before traveling; withdraw the max per session to amortize the fixed fee. *[High]*
- Local operator surcharge is small (~USD 3–6 band); the bigger cost is usually your card's **foreign-transaction fee (~1–3%)** — a no-FX-fee travel card avoids most of it. *[Medium-High for band; copy avoids quoting an exact local figure]*
- **DCC trap:** ATMs/POS offer "charge in your home currency." Always choose **local currency / "without conversion."** Accepting it = double conversion at a bad rate (and can forfeit the IVA benefit). *[High]*
- Sources: guruguay.com/atm-problems-uruguay; flytrippers/onemileatatime (DCC).

### 6.5 Where to get cash — by tourist zone (curated directory; the "locations" core)
Landmark/area level only — **no invented street addresses**.

- **✈ Carrasco International Airport (MVD):** Global Exchange casas de cambio (24/7/365) + a confirmed Santander ATM; HSBC branch reported. Airport exchange rate is poor (~20% worse, single-source → "notably worse") — withdraw a small amount if needed, exchange in the city. *[High for Global Exchange/Santander; Medium for the rest]*
- **⚓ Montevideo:** casas de cambio cluster on Avenida 18 de Julio (Centro) and through Ciudad Vieja; cruise terminal (adjacent to Ciudad Vieja) has an exchange bureau + ATMs at/just outside the port. *[High for 18 de Julio/Ciudad Vieja; Medium for port specifics]*
- **🏖 Punta del Este:** casas de cambio cluster on Avenida Gorlero. High-season (summer + Semana Santa) **cash-shortage risk** — long ATM lines, machines running out of dollars; carry a buffer and lean on cards + Mercado Pago QR. Framed as a recurring risk, **not** a fresh dated event (strongest reporting is 2013; 2025 sources confirm the card/QR shift). *[Medium-High for the phenomenon]*
- **🏰 Colonia del Sacramento:** ATMs in the Barrio Histórico and at the ferry terminal (~100 m from the historic quarter); USD widely accepted at tourist businesses at slightly poor rates. *[Medium]*
- **⛴ Ferry terminals (Buquebus / Colonia Express):** a BROU ATM at the Montevideo Buquebus terminal; ATM in Colonia arrivals; onboard exchange desk reported (~2% fee) — labeled a traveler report, re-verify before publish. *[Low-Medium — expat-forum sourced]*
- **🗺 Interior / smaller towns:** ATMs sparse outside major cities; stock up on cash in Montevideo/Punta del Este/Colonia before heading inland. *[High]*
- Sources: aeropuertodecarrasco.com.uy; Santander bank locator; guruguay.com (exchanging-money, atm-problems); cruise-port guides; baexpats.org; machupicchu.org (Colonia 2026); BCU casas-de-cambio registry.

### 6.6 Casas de cambio vs ATM vs bank
- Main chains: Varlix, Indumex, Gales, Aspen, Cambio Sir, Cambio Inglés, Global Exchange (airport). Best rates for USD (tiny spread), no commission; EUR second, BRL acceptable, ARS poor. *[High]*
- Carry your passport — some branches/amounts ask for ID (AML). *[Medium]*
- Hours: street branches ~Mon–Fri 9–19, Sat morning, many closed Sunday; mall branches open daily and late (e.g., Tres Cruces, Montevideo Shopping). *[High — Varlix store list]*
- Cross-link the existing `/sucursales` page (the site's own casa-de-cambio aggregator).
- Sources: varlix.com.uy/AllShops; guruguay.com/exchanging-money-in-uruguay; BCU registry.

### 6.7 How much cash + which currency to bring
- Bring **USD cash** (best rates, accepted everywhere for exchange); EUR second; **ARS poor** (Argentines: bring USD, not pesos); BRL workable near the Brazil border. *[High; ARS exact figures FX-volatile → directional only]*
- Uruguay is card-friendly/contactless; keep only modest cash for small vendors/transport and pay larger amounts by card. *[High]*
- Sources: guruguay.com; travelwithhello.com; iprofesional (ARS, directional).

### 6.8 Tourist IVA (VAT) benefit — time-boxed
- **Hotels/lodging: 0% IVA for non-residents year-round (permanent)** when paid by foreign card with a foreign ID. *[High]*
- **Restaurants/bars/cafés + car rental:** a 9-point reduction (22%→~13%) as a permanent base, with a **seasonal full 0% exemption that runs roughly each summer (~Nov–Apr)** — most recently 0% for Nov 15 2025–Apr 30 2026 under Decree 220/025. *[High for the rule]*
- ⚠️ **Status flag:** as of June 2026 the seasonal gastronomy/car-rental break is **likely off-season/expired**; the next window would presumably start ~Nov 2026. Copy must present it as "runs each summer — confirm current dates" with a visible **"Last checked: June 2026"** note. Hotel 0% is unaffected. Re-verify against the Ministerio de Turismo page before publish and seasonally thereafter.
- Applied automatically at point of sale (foreign card, non-resident); cash forfeits the benefit → another reason to pay big items by card.
- Sources: gub.uy/ministerio-turismo (beneficios-para-no-residentes); Decreto 220/025 (impo.com.uy); EY Tax News.

### 6.9 Safety
- Use ATMs inside bank glass lobbies / malls, in daylight; be cautious at night or in remote spots. *[High]*
- Watch for motorcycle bag-snatchers near ATMs/parked cars; express kidnappings exist but are uncommon. *[High]*
- Carry 2+ cards from different accounts; split cash, don't carry it all. *[High]*
- Visa/Mastercard near-universal; Amex/Diners patchy. *[Medium — framed qualitatively]*
- Peso denominations: 20/50/100/200/500/1,000/2,000; get smaller bills for taxis/small shops. *[High for denominations]*
- Sources: travel.state.gov; travel.gc.ca; worldnomads; guruguay.com/atm-problems-uruguay.

## 8. Data shape & i18n

- **Page prose →** i18n JSON keys under a `withdraw.*` namespace in `app/i18n/locales/json/{es,en,pt}.json` (matches site convention; gives full trilingual + clean hreflang).
- **Structured catalog →** a new pure-TS module `app/utils/withdrawCash.ts` (like `guides.ts`): typed, language-neutral structures for the zone directory and the network/fee/limit rows, with **inline source + "as of" comments** on staleness-prone values (limits, fees, IVA dates). Labels resolved via i18n keys; the module holds structure/keys, not translated prose.
- Keep the page component thin: it consumes `withdrawCash.ts` + i18n and renders Vuetify sections, mirroring `guias/[slug].vue` and the tools pages.
